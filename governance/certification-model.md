# OpenFrameworks Certification and Accreditation Model

OpenFrameworks certification turns evidence, trust, governance, and reproducible evaluation into formal recognition.

Certification is not marketing. Certification is a governed claim backed by evidence, review, auditability, and lifecycle controls.

## Certification Goals

- identify trustworthy frameworks
- disclose evidence quality
- reduce adoption risk
- support enterprise framework approval
- enable vendor-neutral comparison
- prevent unsupported claims
- make trust portable and auditable

## Certification Subjects

The following asset types may be certified:

- framework
- standard
- protocol
- methodology
- reference architecture
- operating model
- governance model
- maturity model
- benchmark
- blueprint
- implementation pattern
- commercial distribution
- hosted service

## Certification Levels

### OFW-C0: Unverified

No certification. The asset may be listed, but claims are not verified.

### OFW-C1: Registered

The asset has a valid framework card, declared provenance, declared license, and vendor-neutrality section.

### OFW-C2: Evidence Reviewed

The asset has evidence reviewed for source quality, recency, coverage, and conflict disclosures.

### OFW-C3: Governance Approved

The asset has passed governance review, ownership review, neutrality review, and lifecycle review.

### OFW-C4: Benchmark Validated

The asset has reproducible benchmark or assessment results linked to declared criteria.

### OFW-C5: Enterprise Accredited

The asset is suitable for enterprise-approved catalogs when mapped to policy, risk, compliance, and operational controls.

## Certification Requirements

| Level | Required Conditions |
|---|---|
| C1 | Valid schema, provenance, license, neutrality declaration |
| C2 | C1 + evidence review + source diversity |
| C3 | C2 + governance approval + neutrality approval |
| C4 | C3 + reproducible benchmark or assessment |
| C5 | C4 + enterprise controls, compliance mapping, lifecycle owner |

## Badge Vocabulary

- `ofw-c0-unverified`
- `ofw-c1-registered`
- `ofw-c2-evidence-reviewed`
- `ofw-c3-governance-approved`
- `ofw-c4-benchmark-validated`
- `ofw-c5-enterprise-accredited`

## Certification Claim

Every certification claim must include:

- certification level
- issuing authority
- subject asset
- evidence references
- review decision
- reviewer identity
- issue date
- expiry date
- renewal rule
- revocation rule

## Expiry and Renewal

Certification is time-bound.

Recommended defaults:

- C1: 12 months
- C2: 12 months
- C3: 12 months
- C4: 6 months
- C5: 6 months

## Revocation Conditions

Certification may be revoked if:

- evidence is invalidated
- serious incident occurs
- license changes materially
- maintainership becomes inactive
- vendor-neutrality disclosure is incomplete
- conflict of interest is discovered
- benchmark results cannot be reproduced
- governance approval expires

## Non-Negotiable Rules

- No certification without a valid asset card.
- No certification without vendor-neutrality disclosure.
- No certification without evidence references.
- No certification without expiry date.
- No certification without revocation rules.
- Certification does not remove risk; it discloses confidence.
