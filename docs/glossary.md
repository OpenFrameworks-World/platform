# OpenFrameworks Glossary

This glossary defines the controlled language used across OpenFrameworks World.

## Asset

Any governed object that can be identified, described, versioned, related, evaluated, published, or retired.

## Framework

A reusable structure for thinking, building, governing, evaluating, or operating something. A framework may be technical, organizational, educational, operational, regulatory, architectural, or methodological.

## Framework Card

A machine-readable profile that describes a framework's identity, scope, lifecycle, provenance, governance, relationships, evidence, scores, risks, and usage guidance.

## Framework Family

A group of related frameworks that share a domain, lineage, operating model, or adoption context.

## Capability

A measurable ability delivered or enabled by a framework.

## Artifact

A concrete output associated with a framework, such as a schema, template, checklist, report, blueprint, policy, benchmark, implementation guide, or reference architecture.

## Benchmark

A repeatable evaluation method used to assess a framework against criteria, peer frameworks, target states, policies, standards, or operational needs.

## Evidence

A source, observation, test, benchmark, reference, adoption signal, audit result, or expert review used to justify claims about a framework.

## Governance

The policies, roles, approvals, lifecycle rules, review records, exception processes, and audit controls used to manage framework quality and trust.

## Lifecycle State

The governed state of a framework or asset, such as proposed, draft, reviewed, approved, recommended, adopted, deprecated, rejected, or archived.

## Score

A quantitative or qualitative assessment of a framework across defined dimensions such as maturity, security, interoperability, maintainability, documentation, community, adoption, risk, and production readiness.

## Confidence Score

A measure of how much trust should be placed in a framework evaluation based on evidence quality, recency, completeness, and reviewer credibility.

## Risk Score

A measure of possible negative impact, uncertainty, implementation difficulty, governance gaps, security exposure, ecosystem weakness, or maintainability concern.

## Relationship

A typed connection between two assets, such as depends_on, implements, extends, competes_with, complements, replaces, governed_by, or validated_by.

## Blueprint

A composable architecture, operating model, implementation pattern, or solution design assembled from one or more frameworks and assets.

## Recommendation

A governed platform opinion about whether a framework should be adopted, trialed, monitored, avoided, or marked as insufficient evidence.
